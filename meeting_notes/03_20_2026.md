## Notes

- GPT vs Claude score correlation dropped significantly in the latest run.
- One likely cause is rubric over-specificity; the new rubric may be too detailed.
- Claude appears to follow rubric wording very strictly, while GPT is comparatively more lenient.
- Grading a single transcript in isolation is difficult and may increase variance.
- Team wants to test judge context effects by grading transcript bundles instead of only single transcripts.
- Proposed experiments:
  - bundle size 3 transcripts
  - bundle size 6 transcripts
  - multiple composition strategies across persona mixes
- Persona-level visualization shows high variance across persona families.
- Current rubric strictness and section malus deductions may be amplifying cross-model variance.
- Need larger sample size before drawing strong conclusions.

## Decisions

- Prioritize improving GPT-Claude agreement over adding rubric complexity.
- Trim rubric detail where it causes over-constraint.
- Remove section malus deductions to reduce avoidable scoring variance.
- Add bundle-based judging experiments (3 and 6 transcript bundles).
- Scale transcript generation volume to support more statistically meaningful analysis.
- Plan an asynchronous review meeting to evaluate outputs before hand-grade comparison.
