### Grader and rubric calibration

- Verify judge does not use tutor `pedagogical_reasoning` as grading evidence.
- Batch judging prompt should explicitly state that input includes multiple transcripts (bundle mode).
- Largest GPT-Claude disagreement is concentrated in rubric section `3.1`.
- Candidate rubric changes in `3.1`:
  - reduce to 4 points max
  - combine `3.1.A` and `3.1.B`
  - remove highly subjective or redundant items
- Proposed removals due to subjectivity/redundancy:
  - "Message length consistently reduces student opportunity to think and respond."
  - "Tutor keeps explaining at length instead of checking student understanding first."
  - "Unnecessary jargon-heavy wording increases cognitive load."
  - "Word choice is more complex than needed for the student's state."
- Rubric integrity fixes:
  - `1.3.C` label is missing/inconsistent and needs correction.
  - Reword `1.3.A` so rationale/explanation is required at least once per conversation (not every turn).
  - Reword `1.3.D` to evaluate student success after prior failure.
  - For `1.3.D`, test grading with and without this criterion to measure correlation impact.
- Heatmap interpretation discussed:
  - `2.1` appears strong and stable.
  - `1.3` appears generally good.
  - `2.2` and `3.1` show useful signal but still need calibration.
  - `1.2` appears redundant with `3.2` (and partly with `2.2`/`3.1`).
  - strong correlation seen between `1.2` and `3.2`; investigate overlap.
  - inspect coupling around `1.3.B.a` and `2.2.A`.
