### Data security and third-party hosting

- AskTIM stores everything on servers run by Open Learning; main_ui's external site stores everything through Railway, a third-party host. The only difference is where the data lives.
- Plan for the course: keep data on Railway while the course is running, then wipe Railway and store the data internally after the course ends. This addresses the long-term security and privacy question.
- Future-note: any future external-site deployment follows the same pattern — third-party storage during the course, internal migration after.
- Romain will update Dimitris about how we are going about this.
- Double-check that transcripts from students who have not connected an email/student_id are still being stored — we want anonymous-session data preserved, not silently dropped.

- Realistic estimate: about 20 students will use the tutor on and off.
- With a smaller engaged base, end-of-course interviews with users become both feasible and valuable. Future-note: plan to interview students who used the tutor at the end of the course.
