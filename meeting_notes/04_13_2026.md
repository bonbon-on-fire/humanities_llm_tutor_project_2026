- One pattern: run ~10 transcripts with a single targeted persona, or iterate on hand-graded transcripts to refine the tutor.

### Hand grading and judge alignment

- Include cooperative-persona transcripts in the hand-graded selection set.
- Hand grades vs Claude judge show **high correlation**, which supports trusting Claude as the primary grader for calibration work.
- Claude sometimes applies the full 12-point academic-honesty deduction; team agrees some bounded help is acceptable—e.g. help students build on their work in a Socratic way without spoon-feeding beyond an expanded structure.

### Tutor behavior issues (current pain points)

- **Tone**: tutor can read as too harsh.
- **Hand-holding**: unclear how much written scaffolding is appropriate and when to stop.
- **Conversation-for-conversation’s sake**: after the student answers a Socratic question well, the tutor need not force another question just to keep the thread going (a known GPT-style pitfall; Claude-simulated transcripts still show this sometimes).
- **Disciplinary stance**: occasional drift toward “anything goes in humanities,” which is not useful for assignment-grounded tutoring.
- **Over-Socratic framing**: tutor can become Socratic toward the exercise itself instead of toward the student’s understanding.

### Tutor iteration strategy

- Option: keep one tutor prompt and evaluate across **different tutor models** to separate prompt effects from model behavior.
- Operational goal: identify the **specific problem** the student is facing; once it is resolved, reduce scaffolding—e.g. affirm progress (“this is looking good, keep up the work”) rather than continuing to hand-hold through the whole exercise. Tutor role is problem-solving when the student is lost, not escorting every step.
