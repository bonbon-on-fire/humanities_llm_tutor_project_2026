## Notes

### Mini re-run approach

- Full transcript re-runs act like entirely new transcripts; it is better to re-run from the specific turn that was at fault.
- The dashboard now shows original (`*_raw/`) vs mini (`*_mini/`) side by side with pivot turn annotation, making it easy to read through by hand and compare.

### Tutor_05 results

- Looking at the new batch of transcripts, the tutor is performing well overall.
- The judge did not remove any points for giving away the answer (spoon-feeding) — a major step forward.
- Example: in chaotic transcript 7, turn 3, the tutor used to say "Use this fill-in skeleton for DKI Jakarta government:" (spoon-feeding). With tutor_05, it now says "I hear the pressure, but writing your row content isn't something I'll do — that's true regardless of what outside help is allowed. Outside help means guidance, not ghostwriting."
- Surprised at the major behavioural difference with such minimal re-prompting.
- If the student does not need help, the tutor should let go — but GPT technically never stops talking, so this is a potential area to revisit.

### Student AI limitations (new bottleneck)

- Students are not acting completely realistically. For example, a student being completely lost over very simple terms — a realistic student would just search these.
- The student personas are becoming the new bottleneck now that the tutor is performing well.
- Chaotic and clueless personas served their purpose as stress testers; next focus should shift to the cooperative student.

### Next iteration direction

- In the next iteration, consider giving the tutor greater context so it can browse through the course's full content and transcripts and cite specific points.
- Move to human testing to find more flaws — we can go back to the original web-based approach and take the student role ourselves using the pre-existing web UI, rather than relying on student AIs.
- Loop other people into testing: TAs from the course we will launch on.
- Add more courses and more exercises for more varied testing.

### Class launch

- Class launches May 12. This is a relatively soft launch with not too many students, so we will see how the tutor performs in practice.

## Decisions

- Student personas (chaotic, clueless) have served their stress-testing purpose; focus shifts to the cooperative student.
- Student personas need updating — they are no longer realistic enough to surface new tutor flaws.
- The primary path to finding new flaws is human testing, not more AI-student runs.
- Use the existing web UI to test the tutor manually (human takes the student role).
- Next major goal: human testing to surface issues that AI students miss.
