Assignment Overview
This week you will complete a Spider Diagram to map out climate action in your case study city as it relates to the 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation.

The 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation
1. Integrate mitigation and adaptation
2. Coordinate disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation
3. Co-generate risk information
4. Focus on disadvantaged populations
5. Advance governance, finance and knowledge networks
6. Equity and Environmental Justice

The 5 Stages of Progress (climate action status)
1. Assessment
2. Planning
3. Initial Implementation
4. Implementation and Evaluation
5. Full Transformation

Assignment Options
The Spider Diagram activity can be completed in two different ways:
- Option 1: Use the Spider Diagram TABLE (preferred) to write out the information you find about your city.
- Option 2: Use the Spider Diagram DIAGRAM (visual) to draw out the information you find about your city.

After you submit EITHER your Spider Diagram table OR your Spider Diagram diagram to the Graded Response, you will also be required to participate in the Discussion Panel with your fellow learners. Finally, you will give feedback to fellow learners (next week) via peer review.

Before Completing Your Table — Pathway Probes
Refer to your prior research (especially your findings from Week 7). For each pathway, consider:

- Pathway 1 (Integrate mitigation and adaptation): Have you found plans, projects, portfolios or investment commitments that integrate mitigation and adaptation?
- Pathway 2 (Coordinate disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation): Have you found studies, plans or protocols to prepare or recover from disasters that consider the direct implications of climate change?
- Pathway 3 (Co-generate risk information): Who has been engaged in planning/design, decision-making, implementation and/or oversight of climate action?
- Pathway 4 (Focus on disadvantaged populations): Is there an emphasis on the needs/priorities of populations living in vulnerable locations or disproportionately burdened by historic/future environmental impacts?
- Pathway 5 (Advance governance, finance and knowledge networks): Have you found internal/external actors/institutions leveraging technical/financial resources?
- Pathway 6 (Equity and Environmental Justice): Have you found strategies or initiatives, projects or campaigns in your city's planning that address environmental and climate justice and equity?

How to Score Each Pathway
For each Pathway, mark an 'X' in the cell corresponding to the highest stage of progress reached in your city: Assessment, Planning, Initial Implementation, Implementation and Evaluation, or Full Transformation. Marking multiple stages indicates progressive completion (e.g. an X in Assessment, Planning, and Initial Implementation means the city has moved through all three stages).

Spider Diagram (Visual) — Diagram Description

A Spider Diagram is a polar/radial chart with the same information as the table, rendered as a colored web.

Diagram structure (described):
- A circle divided into 6 equal pie-slice segments, one per Pathway.
- 5 concentric rings inside the circle, numbered 1 (innermost) through 5 (outermost), corresponding to the 5 stages of progress (1 = Assessment, 2 = Planning, 3 = Initial Implementation, 4 = Implementation and Evaluation, 5 = Full Transformation).
- Each Pathway slice has a distinct color:
  - Pathway 1 (Integrate mitigation and adaptation): purple
  - Pathway 2 (Coordinate disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation): red
  - Pathway 3 (Co-generate risk information): green
  - Pathway 4 (Focus on disadvantaged populations): yellow
  - Pathway 5 (Advance governance, finance and knowledge networks): cyan
  - Pathway 6 (Equity and Environmental Justice): orange
- For each Pathway slice, you color in the rings from the center outward up to the highest stage your city has reached. E.g. a city at Stage 4 for Pathway 6 would have its orange slice filled from ring 1 through ring 4.

Diagram Instructions
1. Download and print (or work digitally on) the SPIDER DIAGRAM TEMPLATE.
2. For each of the 6 Pathways (one slice per pathway), color in the rings from the center outward to fill the number of stages (1-5) your city has progressed through.
3. The legend on the diagram identifies which color corresponds to which Pathway.
4. Once complete, upload your Spider Diagram to your Graded Response.
