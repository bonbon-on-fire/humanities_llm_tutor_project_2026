Assignment Overview

This week, you will synthesize your findings by conducting a comprehensive SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats) Analysis that evaluates your city's climate action potential across all Urban Pathways.

Understanding SWOT Analysis
SWOT Analysis is a strategic planning tool that helps identify internal and external factors that can impact an organization's — or, in this case, a city's — ability to achieve its goals. For climate action planning, SWOT analysis provides a structured framework to assess:
- Strengths: Internal positive factors that give the city advantages in addressing climate change (existing infrastructure, policies, resources, capabilities)
- Weaknesses: Internal negative factors that hinder climate action (resource limitations, policy gaps, infrastructure deficits)
- Opportunities: External positive factors that the city could leverage for climate action (funding sources, partnerships, emerging technologies, policy changes)
- Threats: External negative factors that could impede progress (economic pressures, political changes, natural disasters, competing priorities)

The 6 Pathways for Urban Transformation
1. Integrate mitigation and adaptation
2. Coordinate disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation
3. Co-generate risk information
4. Focus on disadvantaged populations
5. Advance governance, finance and knowledge networks
6. Equity and Environmental Justice
