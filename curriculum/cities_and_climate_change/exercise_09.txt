Assignment Overview
You will design alternative Climate Action Plan components for your case study city, building on your research from Modules 1 and 2. This is NOT an analysis of existing plans, but your own recommendations based on your city's specific needs.

Step 1: Component Selection
Choose exactly 3 components from the list of Climate Action Plan Components below.

List of Climate Action Plan Components (sourced from UCCRN ARC 3.2, Figure 4: Typical contents of a stand-alone, city-level climate action plan)
1. A long-term VISION STATEMENT, supported by clear objectives and targets, set in short, medium and long-term timescale and grouped into several strategic areas, sectors, or "pillars".
2. An INTRODUCTION, describing how the plan was developed, including public participation processes.
3. A DESCRIPTION of how the climate action plan links with other existing/statutory plans in the city, and other local socio-economic and environmental goals.
4. A DESCRIPTION of how climate action planning links to other national, regional goals, regulations, plans and processes.
5. A TECHNICAL AND SCIENTIFIC SUMMARY including a statement on the science behind climate change and projections of climate impacts, and baseline assessments such as a greenhouse gas emissions inventory, a vulnerability assessment and health implications, or a local renewable energy potential assessment.
6. An OVERVIEW of existing adaptation or mitigation related initiatives.
7. A SUMMARY of how actions were prioritized and other decisions were made, including the criteria used.
8. SETS OF ACTIONS, organized according to several strategic areas with corresponding goals, selected in accordance with criteria, and ensuring co-ordinated action.
9. A STRATEGY FOR OUTREACH, EDUCATION, COMMUNICATION AND DISSEMINATION.
10. A FRAMEWORK FOR REPORTING results and ensuring accountability.
11. A MONITORING AND EVALUATION FRAMEWORK, along with key performance indicators, for measuring progress and updating actions.
12. A GLOSSARY to explain unavoidable technical terms.
13. Simple GRAPHICS used throughout to illuminate key findings, goals, and strategies.

Step 2: Research and Analysis
For each chosen component, write 3-5 bullet points that include:
- Your recommendation for this component
- How your previous case study research supports this choice
- How this addresses your city's specific climate challenges
