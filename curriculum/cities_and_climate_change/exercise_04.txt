Step 2: Submit Your Analysis
You may EITHER submit your Power/Actors Table OR your Power/Actors Map to the Graded Response for peer review. You do not have to submit both. Write a short reflection (200-300 words) on your findings.

Required for: Certificate learners (encouraged for all)

Instructions for Filling Out the Table
- Actor: The roles you've identified as key players in your city. (Example city actors: City Mayor, City Planners, Environmental NGO, Transportation Advocate.)
- Actor Role: One of three options:
  - decision-maker: a key figure with the power to make critical policy-based decisions (e.g. a Mayor)
  - other involved actor: an important figure who influences decision-making but does not make final decisions (e.g. urban planner)
  - relevant but not involved: an important figure who is relevant to climate action but is not currently included in decision-making (e.g. transportation advocate)
- Support of Climate Action: Rank from 0 (Opposed to) to 10 (Strongly in support of) climate action.
- Engagement in Climate Action: Rank from 0 (Not involved at all) to 10 (Very involved in) climate action.
- Actor connected to...: Which other actors in your table is this actor already connected to in climate action work?
- Actor not yet connected to...: Which other actors in your table is this actor NOT yet connected to?

Reading the Example
- The City Mayor is supportive of climate action (7/10) but only moderately engaged (5/10). They are connected to City Planners but not yet to the Environmental NGO, which may have important resources for the Mayor to consider.

- The Transportation Advocate is relevant and engaged but excluded from the formal decision-making network (no connection to Mayor or City Planners).

Power / Actors Map — Diagram (Optional Alternative to the Table)

A visual Power/Actors Map can be used instead of (or in addition to) the table to represent the same information. The diagram is a concentric-circle layout with two axes and color-coded markers.

Diagram structure (described):
- Three concentric circles, from inner to outer:
  - Inner (light grey): Decision Makers
  - Middle (medium grey): Other actors involved
  - Outer (dark grey): Relevant actors uninvolved
- Two axes through the center of the circles:
  - Vertical (North/South) axis: Level of engagement (higher = north, lower = south)
  - Horizontal (East/West) axis: Stance on climate action — Strongly opposing (west) to Strongly supporting (east)
- Markers placed on the map:
  - Blue dots: actors currently involved in climate action in your city
  - Red dots: actors that are missing from the diagram but should be there (i.e. should be involved but are not)
- Connection lines drawn between markers:
  - Solid lines: actors already working together (existing relationships)
  - Dashed lines: new connections that should be made (not working together yet)

Diagram Instructions
1. Review your decision-making actors research and plot each actor on the Power/Actors Map.
2. Plot in BLUE the actors that are currently involved in climate action in your city. Place each one in the circle (light to dark grey) corresponding to their role: decision-maker (inner light grey), other involved actor (middle medium grey), or actor not currently involved (outer dark grey). Label each actor.
3. Plot in RED the actors that are missing from current climate action but should be involved. Label each one.
4. Position each actor by their level of engagement (North = high, South = low) and their stance toward climate action (East = strongly supporting, West = strongly opposing).
5. Draw SOLID lines between actors that are already working together.
6. Draw DASHED lines between actors where a new connection should be made.
7. Assess the match of organizations and actors that have influence over climate action.
