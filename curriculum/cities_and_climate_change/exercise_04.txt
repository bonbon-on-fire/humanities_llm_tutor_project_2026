Instructions for Filling Out the Table

When filling out the table, please use the following as a guide:

- Actor: The roles you've identified as key players in your city. In the example city above, we see the actors of City Mayor, City Planners, Environmental NGO, and Transportation Advocate.
- Actor Role: Can be one of three options:
  - decision-maker: a decision-maker is seen as a key figure in climate action in your city, and someone who has the power to make critical policy-based decisions (i.e. a Mayor)
  - other involved actor: other involved actors are seen as important figures in climate action who are involved in influencing decision-making but who don't have the power to make final decisions (i.e. urban planner)
  - relevant but not involved: relevant but uninvolved actors are seen as important figures in climate action who are not being included in the decision-making process but who are relevant to its progress (i.e. transportation advocates)
  - In the example city above, the City Mayor is considered a decision-maker, as they have significant power in what laws are passed which may protect or harm the environment. The Transportation Advocate, however, is relevant to the discussions around climate action, but has not yet been made more fully involved in climate action-related decisions.
- Opposing / Supporting Climate Action: rank the actor's opposition or support of climate action from 0-10, with 0 being "Opposed to" and 10 being "Strongly in support of" climate action.
- Engagement in Climate Action: rank the actor's engagement in climate action from 0-10, with 0 being "Not involved at all" and 10 being "Very involved in" climate action.
  - In the example city above, the City Planners are both very supportive of and very involved in climate engagement. However, the Mayor, who is still fairly supportive of climate action, is only involved in conversations around climate action about half of the time.
- Actor connected to...: What other actors that you listed is this actor already connected to in terms of climate action?
- Actor not yet connected to...: What other actors that you listed is this actor NOT yet connected to in terms of climate action?
  - In the example city above, we see that the Mayor is connected to the City Planners, but has not yet been connected to the environmental NGO, which may have important resources for the mayor to consider when making decisions.

Power / Actors Map — Diagram (Optional Alternative to the Table)

A visual Power/Actors Map can be used instead of (or alongside) the table to represent the same information. The diagram is a concentric-circle layout with two axes and color-coded markers.

Image: see figures/exercise_04_power_actors_map.png in this course folder for the visual reference diagram.

Diagram structure (described):
- Three concentric circles, from inner to outer:
  - Inner (light grey): Decision Makers
  - Middle (medium grey): Other actors involved
  - Outer (dark grey): Relevant actors uninvolved
- Two axes through the center of the circles:
  - Vertical (North/South) axis: Level of engagement (higher engagement = north / top, lower engagement = south / bottom)
  - Horizontal (East/West) axis: Stance on climate action — Strongly opposing (west / left) to Strongly supporting (east / right)
- Markers plotted on the map:
  - Blue dots: actors currently involved in climate action in your city
  - Red dots: actors that are missing from the diagram but should be there (i.e. should be involved but are not)
- Connection lines drawn between markers:
  - Solid lines: actors already working together (existing relationships)
  - Dashed lines: new connections that should be made (not working together yet)

Below is an example of a completed map, for an example city. The web of connection lines creates a visual picture of how the actors are connected (or not) to others who may pull them toward more climate action. In the example, four blue dots are placed (three in the inner two circles, indicating decision-makers and other involved actors, and one in the outer ring as a relevant actor) and one red dot in the outer ring on the opposing side, indicating a missing actor that should be involved. Solid green lines connect actors already working together; dashed red lines indicate where new connections should be made.

Diagram Instructions
Follow the steps below to complete your Power/Actors Map Diagram, using the downloadable template provided in your course materials.

1. Review your decision-making actors research and use this to plot these actors on the Power/Actors Map.
2. Plot in BLUE the actors that are currently involved in climate action in your city. Place them in the map's circles (light to dark grey) according to whether they are decision-makers (light grey inner), other involved actors (medium grey middle), or actors not currently involved (dark grey outer). Label these actors in the diagram.
3. Plot in RED the actors that are missing from the diagram but should be there. Label these actors in the diagram.
4. Plot actors based on their level of engagement (North/South part of the diagram) as well as whether they are in favor of or oppose climate action (East/West part of the diagram). Label these actors in the diagram.
5. Next, assess the connections and power dynamics between decision-making actors. Use SOLID lines to connect actors that are already working together, and DASHED lines to highlight new connections that should be made.
6. Assess the match of organizations and actors that have influence over climate action.
